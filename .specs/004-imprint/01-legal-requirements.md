# Legal Requirements - German Imprint Law

## Legal Framework

### DDG Section 5 (formerly TMG Section 5)

The **Telemediengesetz (TMG)** was repealed on **May 14, 2024** and replaced by the **Digitale-Dienste-Gesetz (DDG)**. The imprint obligation is now in **DDG Section 5**. The substantive requirements are unchanged — same rules, new law name.

**Important:** Do not reference specific law sections in the imprint text itself (e.g., "Angaben gemaess DDG Section 5") to avoid needing updates when laws change again.

### Who Needs an Imprint?

DDG Section 5 applies to providers of digital services offered **"geschaeftsmassig"** (on a sustained, organized basis to the public). This is NOT the same as "gewerblich" (commercial):

- **Geschaeftsmassig** = offered on a sustained, recurring basis to the public, regardless of money
- **Gewerblich** = for profit, with commercial intent

A site does NOT need to earn money to be geschaeftsmassig. The threshold is about *how* the site operates, not *why*.

### Assessment for This Project

The Mini-Z Leaderboard is:
- Publicly accessible (not restricted to family/friends)
- Regularly updated with new race data
- Providing a structured service to a community
- Non-profit, no ads, no commercial elements

**Verdict:** The site is very likely geschaeftsmassig due to being publicly accessible and regularly maintained. A purely private exemption only applies to sites limited to family/friends (e.g., password-protected family photo albums). An imprint is recommended.

**However, the practical risk of enforcement is very low:**
- UWG Abmahnungen require a competitive relationship — a non-commercial hobby site has no competitors in the legal sense
- Regulatory authorities focus on commercial sites
- The 2020 "Abmahnmissbrauch" reforms (UWG Section 8c) raised the bar significantly for small operators
- There are very few documented cases of purely non-commercial hobby sites being targeted

**Bottom line:** Include an imprint to be safe, but keep it **minimal** — both for privacy and because the legal risk is low.

## Required Content (Minimal for Private Individual)

| Field | Required? | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Full name** (first + last) | Yes | Cannot be avoided if imprint applies |
| **Postal address** | Yes | Must be a real address where mail reaches you. **A c/o address or mail forwarding service is accepted** (e.g., Clevvermail, ~5-15 EUR/month). PO boxes are NOT accepted. |
| **Email address** | Yes | Sufficient as "rapid electronic contact" |
| **Phone number** | **No** | ECJ ruling C-298/17 established that email alone suffices as rapid electronic contact. Optional. |

### What You Do NOT Need (for a private non-commercial hobby site)

- **Section 18 MStV "Verantwortlich fuer den Inhalt"** — only for journalistic/editorial content. A data leaderboard is not editorial.
- **Commercial register number** (Handelsregisternummer) — only for registered businesses
- **VAT identification number** (Umsatzsteuer-ID) — only for commercial entities
- **Supervisory authority** — only for regulated professions
- **Professional title / chamber** — only for regulated professions

### Disclaimer Sections

Disclaimers (Haftungsausschluss) for content and links are **optional**. They are common on German websites but have limited legal effect. For a minimal imprint, they can be omitted entirely. If included, keep them brief.

## Privacy Considerations

### GDPR vs Impressumspflicht

There is a tension between GDPR's data minimization principle (Art. 5(1)(c)) and the imprint obligation. The resolution:

- The imprint is a **legal obligation** under Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR — publishing the required data is GDPR-compliant
- But this supports publishing **only the minimum required** — don't over-share
- No phone number if email suffices
- Use a c/o address to protect your home address

### Protecting Your Home Address

For a private individual, publishing a home address on a public website is a real privacy concern. **Recommended alternatives:**

1. **c/o mail forwarding service** (~5-15 EUR/month) — provides a business address you can legally use in your imprint. Mail is forwarded to you. Widely accepted as compliant.
2. **c/o a friend or family member** — if they agree, you can use their address with a c/o prefix. Must be a real address where mail reaches you.

A PO box (Postfach) is **not** accepted — courts require a "ladungsfaehige Anschrift" (address where process can be served).

## Accessibility Requirements

German law requires the imprint to be:

1. **"Leicht erkennbar"** (easily identifiable) — labeled clearly as "Impressum"
2. **"Unmittelbar erreichbar"** (directly accessible) — max 2 clicks from any page
3. **"Staendig verfuegbar"** (constantly available) — on every page, not behind auth

A persistent footer link satisfies all three.

## Language Requirements

- **German is mandatory** for Germany-based sites
- English translation is recommended for bilingual sites but legally secondary
- The link label **must** be **"Impressum"** (German) / **"Legal Notice"** (English)
